LEARN. TEACH. GROW. REPEAT.

Learn.

30% of web developers are women, and women of color represent just a fraction of that number. By 2020, there will be 1.4 million tech jobs available and you don't need an Ivy League computer science degree to get in. Our focus is not on a particular technology, rather members come with their own tech stack -- or a burning desire to learn.

Teach | Grow.

Our learning model is built on the belief that the best way to understand a concept is to explain it to someone else. “While we teach, we learn,” said the Roman philosopher Seneca. It's called The Protégé Effect. Our members come from different backgrounds, either self-taught or college educated, and as we learn new technologies, we share it with others. This way, everyone grows!

Repeat.

The one constant in life is change. In technology this is more true than in any other profession. As we go on in our careers, change will occur continually. This is why learning is a continual process. There is always an opportunity to expand our skillsets and then in turn mentor the next generation of engineers to encourage innovation.
