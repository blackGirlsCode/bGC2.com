Who We Are

Our members come from different backgrounds, either self-taught or college educated, all have a unique story that has brought them together. The common thread that binds us is to seek likeness in another and a burning desire to create our very own versions of disruptive technology.

Our Mission

Charlotte, NC is our birthplace, but be advised, we won't stop here. Women of color represent just a small fraction of the technical workforce and that extends globally. bGC2 exists to connect and build the community of women of color in technology and close the gap in the digitial divide.

How we do it.

Technology is only as good as the developers making it happen. By learning together and sharing knowledge growth is a continal process - as it should be. Each member has a dual role of student and mentor not just within the bGC(2) sphere, but also within the communities in which we live.

Some Guidelines

  • Don't take yourself too seriously.
  • Be of equal service to yourself and the community.
  • Only stupid questions are those that aren't asked.
  • Fail miserable totally and completely but always forward.
  • Know you are limitless. Test and stretch your limitations and when you feel your potential has been met, stretch even more.
  • When the going gets tough and you want to quit, see it as a sign you're on the right path.

Erica Mitchell

Founder, blackGirlsCode(2)

Founding BGC2 is one of the most ambitious initiatives I've taken to date. Like many women and girls, Software Developers that look like me are hard to come by. Before learning to code, what I imagined a Sm toftware Engineer is did not look like me and the thought to pursie a technology never eve crossed my mind, and writing my first line of code was -- scary. What I realized, though is my knack for solving problems is much better served through code. I have not looked back since and followed the signs from the Universe to establish bGC(2) to seek out other ladies with similar interests.

The vision of bGC(2) continually evolves as new members come for coffee && code, or when someone shares a stellar idea. When my head isn't buried in a laptop, my time is occupied with my four kids. A few of my favorite things in life are classic literature, Italian horror films, and stuffing my face with all the unique dining available here in Charlotte. You can find my musings on Medium and the latest projects, and technnology I'm learning at ericamitchellonline.com
